Send today. Customize the greeting per organization.

**Subject:** Quick student hackathon question about immigration notice support

Hi [Name / Team],

I'm a student at Seattle University. My team is competing in an Amazon/AWS-sponsored hackathon this weekend (May 16–18, 2026).

We're building a prototype called **Carta Clara** — an iPhone app that helps Spanish-speaking immigrant families understand frightening English-language notices in plain Spanish, with a printable "preparation packet" they can bring to a free legal-aid appointment.

We are **not** building an AI lawyer. The app refuses legal-strategy questions and routes users to qualified human help. We're using AWS Bedrock's Guardrails to enforce these refusals visibly.

Before we present, we'd love 5–10 minutes of discovery feedback from someone who works with immigrant clients. We're not asking for endorsement, your client information, or use of your name in our pitch unless you explicitly offer it. We just want to make sure the tool is safe, respectful, and actually useful.

Five questions, by email or phone, whichever is easier:

1. What immigration documents most often scare or confuse the people you serve?
2. What should an AI tool like this absolutely never say?
3. Would a plain-Spanish summary + deadline extraction + scam/notario red flags be useful *before* someone speaks with you?
4. What scams or notario red flags do you commonly see in 2026?
5. What wording would make this product feel safe instead of dangerous?

Even one paragraph back would shape how we build it.

Thank you for the work you do,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
Seattle University
