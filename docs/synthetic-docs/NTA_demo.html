NOTICE TO RESPONDENT

Warning: Any statement you make may be used against you in removal proceedings.

Right to Representation: You have the right to be represented at no expense to the Government by counsel of your own choice. If you wish to be represented, your attorney must file Form EOIR-28 with the Immigration Court. A list of qualified attorneys and organizations who may provide free legal services is available upon request from the Immigration Court.

Right to Hearing: At your hearing, you may present evidence on your own behalf, examine evidence against you, and question witnesses presented by the Government.

Right to Appeal: After the immigration judge issues a decision, you have the right to appeal to the Board of Immigration Appeals within thirty (30) days.

Failure to Appear: Failure to attend your hearing, except for exceptional circumstances, will result in your being held in absentia and an order of removal being entered against you. You will be ineligible for certain forms of relief from removal (8 U.S.C. § 1229a(b)(7)) for a period of ten (10) years following the date of entry of the final administrative order of removal.

Interpreter: You will be provided an interpreter at your hearing at no cost. If you require an interpreter, notify the Immigration Court before your hearing date.
